    *   **Loan Level:** Increasing the proportion of high-yield, risk-controllable loans, potentially replacing some lower-yield loans.

    *   **Stable Asset Quality:** The overall NPL ratio for corporate loans in 2025 was stable at 0.54%, with an NPL formation rate of around 0.3%, providing a solid foundation.
    *   **Rational Pricing:** Broader industry self-regulation has reduced intense price competition, leading to a more rational pricing environment.
    *   **Front-Loaded Quality Projects:** A significant portion of the annual corporate lending target, comprising high-quality projects prepared in late 2025, was deployed in Q1. This "early lending, early return" strategy secured better pricing and quality.
2.  **Retail Loans:** While the retail side is under more pricing pressure, there are still opportunities for higher-yield loans where risks can be controlled through refined customer segmentation and process optimization.

A: The positive trend is due to a combination of proactive risk management and improving asset quality indicators:
1.  **Process Optimization:** Since 2024, the bank has conducted a comprehensive review and optimization of its micro-loan processes, including tightening access criteria and improving customer segmentation. This has significantly improved the quality of new customers.
2.  **Improved Asset Quality of New Loans:** As a result of these measures, the 90+ day overdue ratio by the 15th month for newly approved micro-loan customers has dropped significantly, from a peak of over 2% to just over 1%.
